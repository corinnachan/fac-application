Why Now

I am hugely passionate about encouraging more women and under-represented groups to become developers, and aspire to be a role model for those who join this career from non-traditional paths after I do so myself. After many years of working adjacently to developers in my career and teaching myself to code in a hobbyist capacity, I am keen to build a foundation where I could realistically pursure developer roles in a professional capacity. Scroll down to learn more about my coding journey to date and key people who have inpsired me along the way!

My Coding Journey

ShePlusPlus London | Dec 2017

I have always dreamed of being a personal website, but had no idea where to start. ShePlusPlus introduced me to the wonderful world of GitHub Pages, and I finally made some progress on building my own site (using Jekyll)!

Visit Site →

Rails Girls London | Dec 2018

This weekend was pivotal for two reasons: 1) I learned about @codingwithclair's incredible journey of being a self-taught coder and pivoted her career from advertising into software engineering; 2) I learned about codebar, which became an intrumental support network in my own coding journey. At codebar I also met a FAC alumna who highly recommended FAC to me, and the idea has stuck with me since! I continued to teach myself HTML, CSS, and JavaScript throughout 2019.

Never Too Old to Be A Junior Again @ PyLadies Dublin | Oct 2023

After a long hiatus during the COVID years to prioritise reconnecting with people IRL, I was re-inspired to consider a career in development again at PyLadies Dublin, where Melina Zhang gave an in-person talk on her career change at age 41 to become a junior dev.
